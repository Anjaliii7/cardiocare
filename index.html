Did you know ?

Here are some interesting facts about heart health:

Understanding these facts can help you make informed decisions about your heart health.


The Heart‚Äôs Strength and rarity of Cancer.

Your heart is a powerful pump‚Äîeach minute, it circulates around 1.5 gallons (5.7 liters) of blood throughout your body. Over time, this flow adds up: in less than a year, your heart could pump enough blood to fill an Olympic-sized swimming pool. This constant effort highlights just how tirelessly the heart works to keep you alive and healthy. Heart cancer is extremely rare because the heart is composed mostly of cells called cardiomyocytes, which stop dividing shortly after birth. Most cancers form in tissues with rapidly dividing cells, making them more prone to mutations. Since heart cells rarely divide, there's a lower chance for such mutations to occur. Additionally, the heart‚Äôs constant motion and rich blood flow may create an environment less favorable for tumor growth. As a result, primary heart cancers like cardiac sarcomas are exceptionally uncommon.

Heart disease is the leading cause of death globally.

It accounts for nearly 18 million deaths each year. The most common type is coronary artery disease, which restricts blood flow to the heart. Risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity, diabetes, and a sedentary lifestyle. Many heart diseases are preventable with early lifestyle changes, regular checkups, and awareness of symptoms like chest pain or shortness of breath.

Laughing is good for the heart and can increase blood flow by 20 percent.

Laughter is great for heart health. It improves blood flow by up to 20%, relaxes blood vessels, and reduces stress hormones like cortisol. Laughing also increases oxygen intake, relaxes muscles, lowers inflammation, and boosts the immune system. Plus, it strengthens social bonds, which are linked to better heart health. In short, laughter supports both physical and emotional well-being.

Smoking doubles the risk of a heart attack.

In fact, smokers are twice as likely to suffer a heart attack compared to non-smokers. The harmful chemicals in tobacco damage the lining of blood vessels, raise blood pressure, reduce oxygen in the blood, and cause the heart to work harder. Even exposure to secondhand smoke can increase heart disease risk. Quitting smoking greatly improves heart health and reduces the risk within just a few years.

Walking 30 minutes a day lowers heart risk.

Regular brisk walking helps improve circulation, reduce blood pressure, manage weight, and boost heart function. It also lowers bad cholesterol (LDL) and increases good cholesterol (HDL). Consistent daily walking is one of the simplest and most effective ways to support long-term heart health.

Nutrition

Eat heart-friendly foods that fuel your body and boost cardiovascular strength.

Nutrition is a critical part of health and development. Better nutrition is related to improved infant, child and maternal health, stronger immune systems, safer pregnancy and childbirth, lower risk of non-communicable diseases (such as diabetes and cardiovascular disease), and longevity. Healthy children learn better. People with adequate nutrition are more productive and can create opportunities to gradually break the cycles of poverty and hunger. Malnutrition, in every form, presents significant threats to human health.

Workouts

Do simple, effective exercises to keep your heart strong and energy high every day.

Regular physical activity improves blood circulation, strengthens your heart, and significantly reduces stress levels. Just 30 minutes a day of moderate movement ‚Äî whether it's brisk walking, cycling, yoga, or dancing ‚Äî can greatly enhance your cardiovascular health. Exercise helps regulate blood pressure, boosts mood by releasing endorphins, and improves sleep quality. It also assists in maintaining a healthy weight, reducing the risk of obesity, type 2 diabetes, and high cholesterol ‚Äî all major contributors to heart disease.

Regular Checkups

Regular health screenings help detect heart issues early, enabling timely treatment

Routine health checkups play a crucial role in maintaining heart health, even when no symptoms are visible. Many heart-related conditions, such as high blood pressure or elevated cholesterol, develop silently over time. Regular screenings ‚Äî including blood pressure measurement, cholesterol level analysis, blood sugar tests, and ECGs (electrocardiograms) ‚Äî help detect issues early before they become serious. Early diagnosis allows for timely lifestyle changes or medical interventions that can prevent complications like heart attacks or strokes.

Stress Management

A peaceful mind is the first step toward a powerful, healthy heart.

Chronic stress is a silent threat to heart health. It triggers the release of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which over time can lead to high blood pressure, irregular heart rhythms, and even cardiovascular disease. Prolonged stress also encourages unhealthy coping mechanisms such as overeating, smoking, or lack of physical activity, which further strain the heart. Incorporating stress-reduction techniques into your daily routine can significantly improve your well-being. Simple practices like meditation, mindful breathing, journaling, or yoga calm the mind and reduce heart strain.

Hydration

Stay hydrated for optimal heart function.

Drinking enough water plays a vital role in maintaining a healthy cardiovascular system. Proper hydration helps regulate blood volume, supports smooth circulation, and ensures that essential nutrients and oxygen are efficiently delivered to organs‚Äîincluding the heart. Dehydration, on the other hand, can cause blood to thicken, forcing the heart to work harder and potentially leading to fatigue or dizziness. To keep your heart functioning optimally, aim for at least 8 glasses (about 2 liters) of water daily. If you're physically active or in a hot climate, your body may need even more. A hydrated body means a well-supported heart‚Äîso sip regularly throughout the day for steady energy, better focus, and improved heart performance.
